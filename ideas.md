# Jericho Hall Website — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Jericho Hall is the reigning champion hall at New Saint Andrews College (NSA), a classical Christian liberal arts college in Moscow, Idaho. Colors: **deep forest green** and **old gold**. The hall's identity draws from the biblical Battle of Jericho — valor, faith, triumph. The website must feel timeless, classy, and not dated.

## Idea 1: Classical Heraldic — "The Hall of Valor"

**Design Movement:** English Heraldic Revival meets Oxbridge collegiate tradition

**Core Principles:**
1. Gravitas through restraint — nothing extraneous, every element earns its place
2. Heraldic geometry — crests, shields, banners, and ruled lines as structural motifs
3. Parchment warmth — cream and gold grounds that feel aged but not worn
4. Typographic authority — serif display fonts that command respect

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: warm off-white / parchment (#F5F0E8)
- Primary: deep forest green (oklch equivalent of #2D5016)
- Accent: old gold (#B8960C)
- Text: near-black charcoal (#1A1A14)
- Emotional intent: dignity, permanence, earned authority

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric editorial layout: large left-anchored hero text with right-side crest
- Horizontal ruled lines (thin gold) as section dividers
- Content in wide columns with generous margins, not centered boxes
- Scoreboard as a heraldic "ledger" table with ruled borders

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin gold horizontal rules above section headings
2. Crest/shield motif repeated as decorative watermarks in section backgrounds
3. Roman numeral year stamps and "Est. MMXXI" typography

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Subtle fade-in on scroll (no bouncing or flashy transitions)
- Hover states: gold underline reveals on links
- Video thumbnails: slight desaturation until hover

**Animation:**
- Entrance: sections fade up with 0.4s ease-out
- No parallax — timeless, not trendy
- Scoreboard numbers count up on first view

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (bold, italic for emphasis)
- Body: EB Garamond (11pt equivalent, generous line-height)
- Labels/caps: Cormorant SC (small caps for section labels)

## Idea 2: Dark Collegiate — "Fortis et Fidelis"

**Design Movement:** Dark Academia meets Military Heraldry

**Core Principles:**
1. Deep, rich darkness — forest green and near-black as primary surfaces
2. Gold as the only light — gold text and accents glow against dark grounds
3. Architectural gravitas — columns, arches, and ruled frames
4. Manuscript texture — subtle paper grain over dark surfaces

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: very dark green-black (oklch(0.12 0.04 145))
- Surface: dark forest green (oklch(0.22 0.07 145))
- Gold: old gold (oklch(0.72 0.15 85))
- Text: warm cream (oklch(0.92 0.02 85))
- Emotional intent: strength, mystery, tradition, dominance

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed dark hero with centered crest and large gold display type
- Side-by-side card layouts with dark green cards and gold borders
- Scoreboard as a dramatic lit "leaderboard" on dark ground
- Navigation: fixed top bar with gold logo and minimal links

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin gold border frames on cards and section headers
2. Eagle/crest watermark at very low opacity in dark backgrounds
3. Gold horizontal dividers with small diamond ornaments at center

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover: gold border brightens and box shadow expands
- Active states: gold fill
- Video section: dark overlay with gold play button

**Animation:**
- Hero: crest fades in with slow scale (1.05 → 1.0) over 1.2s
- Section entrance: slide-up 30px + fade, staggered 0.1s per card
- Scoreboard: numbers animate upward like an odometer

**Typography System:**
- Display: Cinzel (Roman capitals, authoritative)
- Body: Crimson Text (elegant serif, excellent readability on dark)
- Accent: Cinzel Decorative for hall name only

## Selected Design: **Idea 2 — Dark Collegiate "Fortis et Fidelis"**

This approach best matches the request for "timeless and classy" while honoring Jericho Hall's green-and-gold identity. The dark collegiate aesthetic has deep roots in British and American university tradition (Oxford, Yale, West Point) and will not look dated. Gold on dark green is inherently elegant and commands authority — fitting for a three-time champion hall.

**Key decisions:**
- Dark forest green backgrounds with old gold accents
- Cinzel for display type (Roman authority)
- Crimson Text for body (scholarly warmth)
- Thin gold border frames on all cards
- Eagle/crest watermark motifs
- Scoreboard as a dramatic lit leaderboard
